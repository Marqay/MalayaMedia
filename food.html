Traditional Food

Lontong

A traditional Southeast Asian dish made from compressed rice cake, usually cut into small cubes and served with a variety of side dishes. It’s often enjoyed with vegetable curry (sayur lodeh), peanut sauce, boiled eggs, or rendang.

Lepat Pisang

A traditional Malaysian snack made from mashed bananas mixed with grated coconut and a touch of sugar, wrapped in banana leaves, and steamed to perfection.

Tepung Pelita

A traditional Malaysian dessert known for its two-layered, creamy texture. The bottom layer is made from sweet pandan-flavored rice flour, while the top layer is rich coconut milk with a slightly salty taste creating a perfect balance of flavors.

Nona Manis Pandan

A Malaysian dessert featuring a soft pandan-flavored layer topped with a rich, creamy coconut custard. It has a sweet and fragrant taste, with the natural aroma of pandan giving it a refreshing tropical flavor.

Panipuri

A famous street food snack, loved for its crispy, hollow puris filled with a flavorful mix of spiced water (pani), mashed potatoes, chickpeas, and tamarind chutney.

Chapati

A traditional unleavened flatbread made from whole wheat flour, water, and a pinch of salt. It’s cooked on a hot griddle until soft and slightly browned, often brushed with a bit of oil or ghee for extra flavor.

Idli

A steamed rice cake made from a fermented batter of rice and urad dal (black lentils). Soft, fluffy, and light, idlis are a healthy breakfast favorite across India and Malaysia.

Roti Naan

A soft and fluffy flatbread traditionally baked in a tandoor (clay oven), giving it a slightly smoky flavor and crispy edges. Made from wheat flour, yogurt, and yeast, pairs perfectly with butter chicken, tandoori dishes, or rich curries.

Mooncake

A traditional Chinese enjoyed during the Mid-Autumn Festival, symbolizing reunion and harmony. It has a rich, dense filling and often made from lotus seed paste, red bean paste, or mixed nuts also sometimes contains a salted egg yolk representing the full moon.

Dimsum

A Chinese cuisine consisting of a variety of small bite-sized dishes, usually served in bamboo steamers or small plates.

Tang Yuan

A traditional Chinese dessert made of glutinous rice balls, often filled with sweet sesame, peanut, or red bean paste, and served in a warm, lightly sweetened syrup or ginger soup.

Kuih Bakul

A traditional sticky rice cake made from glutinous rice flour and brown sugar. It has a sweet, chewy texture and is often steamed in banana-leaf-lined containers.
