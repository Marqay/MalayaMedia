Malaysia’s 68th National Day reception in Brunei celebrated friendship and shared heritage.

The Asia Food Fest celebrates the variety of halal food from across Asia. Held at Jaya Shopping Centre, it gathers different Asian cuisines such as Japanese, Thai, and Malaysian dishes. The event promotes cultural harmony, encourages people to explore regional flavours, and strengthens appreciation for Asia’s rich culinary traditions.

The Malaysia Food & Cultural Festival aims to promote Malaysia’s rich food and cultural heritage in Chennai, India. Organized by the Consulate General of Malaysia and Tourism Malaysia, the event strengthens diplomatic ties between both countries.

The Havoc Food Festival brings together local and international food brands in a fun, colourful environment at Setia City Convention Centre. Supported by Tourism Malaysia, it offers visitors food, music, and entertainment. The festival supports local businesses, attracts families and youth, and promotes Malaysia’s vibrant food culture and tourism industry.

The Big Food Festival featuring Ubi Lambung brings Malaysians together through their shared love of food. With colorful flavors like pandan and pumpkin, it celebrates the nation’s diversity and creativity. The event strengthens community bonds, supports local vendors, and reflects Malaysia’s unity in diversity
